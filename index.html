Dating App Trends

Created by Laura Smith

In 2024, over 360 million people from around the world used at least one dating app and about 53% of people have a positive experience with it!

In 2023, Morgan Stanley found that the average paying user pays about $19 per month, with some people spending hundreds of dollars across multiple platforms.

Paying members make up over one-third of dating app users.

A study of over 1,000 Americans who had met their current partner on a dating app found that, on average, they spend over 8 months swiping nearly 4,000 times before they find someone.

With the help of this, the dating app industry brought in over $6.18 billion in revenue last year, globally.

Over 50% of this was by one company, Match Group, who owns Tinder.

About 60% of all people using Tinder in 2020 were looking for a long-term or serious relationship.

A Forbes study found that online dating apps is the most common way most age groups met people to date in 2023.

And of people who have ever used a dating app, 4 in 10 of them met their current partner or spouse through one.

“Every ‘match’ results in a dopamine boost. The gamification of dating apps occurs when single people begin to use dating apps to feel better about themselves rather than for their intended use.”

-Dr. Stacey Litam, a licensed professional clinical counselor, sexologist, and Forbes Health Advisory Board member.
