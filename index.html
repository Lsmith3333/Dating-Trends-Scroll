Dating App Trends

Created by Laura Smith

Many people question the capability of finding
love online, as well as the strength of any
relationship found there.

But, in 2024, over 360 million people from around
the world used at least one dating app and about
53% of people have a positive experience with them!

In 2023, Morgan Stanley found that the average
paying user pays about $19 per month, with some
people spending hundreds of dollars across
multiple platforms.

Paying members make up over one-third of dating app users.

A study of over 1,000 Americans who had met their
current partner on a dating app found that,
on average, they spend over 8 months swiping
nearly 4,000 times before they find someone.

With the help of this, the dating app industry
brought in over $6.18 billion in revenue last year, globally.

Over 50% of this was by one company,
Match Group, who owns Tinder.

About 60% of all people using Tinder in 2020 were
looking for a long-term or serious relationship,
contrary to the typical reputation that Tinder holds.

A Forbes study found that online dating apps is
the most common way most age groups met
people to date in 2023.

And of people who have ever used a dating app,
4 in 10 of them met their current partner
or spouse through one.

Meanwhile, The Knot reported that in their 2024
study, 27% of their engaged couples met on
an app, most frequently Hinge.

Just like any other dating method, dating apps
work great for some people, and less well for
others. It is important to join an app that fits your
personality and interests.
